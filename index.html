Overview

Purpose

This website is intended to provide information about our services and give an overview of our company that places our service and prices above any other rafting company. Our purpose is to involve potential customers visually, through images of satisfied groups and families while making use of our products and services. It is also important to include authentic photos on the lakes, photos that convey a positive message. Advertisements with tour promotions with special discounts for families and groups and reviews from our clients will also be essential in the presentation of our site to guarantee a good number of customers.

Audience

Our main target audience will be groups of people, that is families, communities of company workers, students, and religious groups. It includes young people and adults between 10 and 60 years old, approximately, both residents in Chile and tourists, who browse the web by using a phone or a desktop computer in search of adventure that involve sport activities such as white water rafting, kayaking, and canyoning at a moderate cost and with a high_quality service.

Typography

Heading Font: Original Surfer

Original Surfer is a font from google that loads quickly. It will be used for the titles since it is striking, modern, and fun, but at the same time, it is clear, clean, and easy to read.

Paragraph Font: Oranienbaum

This is a type of font that is easy to read, and adds modernity and professionalism to the website. It also matches very well with the font of the headlines.

The Site Map of a site is just like it sounds…it is a map of the pages in a site and how they are related and linked together. From the map above we can see that we will eventually have the Home page and 2 sub or child pages.

The lines that connect them all together indicate that each page should be accessible from any other page, it is essentially showing us the global navigation for the site.

Wireframes

Wireframes are like blueprints for making webpages. They should show the major sections of content that will be on the page and the relative locations of each element. In the wireframe below you can see there will be 6 sections to our page:

  1. At the top we have a section with the logo (the box with the mountain means an image) and the navigation bar.
  2. Then there is a banner image that stretches all the way across the screen.
  3. Next we have some text and an image
  4. ...followed by another row made up of an image and some text.
  5. Then one more section of text with no image.
  6. Lastly, a footer containing a copyright/name line and 3 social media icons.
